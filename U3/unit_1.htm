Unit 1

Learning aim A

Investigate online services and online communication

Online services Understand how and why online services can be used. Examples of online services, include:

  • commerce (internet banking, online auction websites, retail sales, publishing)
  • real-time information (train timetables, news services, traffic reports, flight status updates, weather)
  • communication (email, instant messaging, newsgroups, social networking, online conferencing, blogs, chat room)
  • government (online tax returns, e-voting, applications for services/grants, revenue collection)
  • education (online learning/training)
  • virtual learning environments (VLEs)
  • business (video conferencing, collaborative working, business networks)
  • entertainment (multi-user games, radio players)
  • download services (music, film, upgrades, software).

Online communication Understand how and why online communication can be used, including:

  • contemporary social media to post, publish and access information, including web logs (blogs), wikis and podcasting
  • virtual learning environments (VLE) in education
  • social networking websites to share information and build online communities. Recognise and use appropriate key terms in online communication:
  • chatroom.
  • netiquette
  • profile
  • network of friends
  • online community
  • virtual world.
